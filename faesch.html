Related Hits Explanation

The displayed related hits are calculated by using a measure of semantic similarity.
In a first step, the title of the work is transformed into a mathematical representation in the form of a vector (e.g. by an algorithm like Doc2Vec).
Next, a similarity score between the work and all other works is attained by calculating the cosinus similarity between them. The five highest scores are then displayed here.

The implementation currently suffers from the problem that the model used to create the vectors of the titles is not specialized on latin or premodern german texts, so it mainly takes into account matching parts of words and less the meaning of those words or the titles in general.

Due to the fact that the vector is calculated only based on the title, it does not take into account information filtered out previously like the author or publishing date. We decided on this strategy as works by the same author for example can easily be found throug the search filter.
